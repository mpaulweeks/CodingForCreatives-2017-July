Don't Make Me Think Notes

  • This week I read five chapters of "Don't Make Me Think," by Steve Krug. He stresses his motto: "don't make me think." Go figure.

    Essentially, webpages should be intuitive; you shouldn't be trying to figure what goes where or confused by what you're seeing. They can be intuitive by using good:

    • Design Elements Such as Heirarchy
    • Concise Language and Eliminating Extra Words
    • Being intuitive

    The reason we want to be this way is because much like "good lighting in a museum," when websites are designed right, it makes everything seem better.

    There's also a lot of emphasis on the fact that humans tend to "scan" websites, not read them. Humans don't go over every single option and then carefully click the best one - they find the first closest option that they can, then go with that (satisficing).
