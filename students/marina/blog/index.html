DON’T MAKE ME THINK, REVISITED

A Common Sense Approach to Web Usability

Don’t Make Me Think is a great book written by Steve Krug that help to understand the principles of intuitive navigation and information design of web usability.

Krug uses plain language to describe how to make things easy to use, which makes life a lot easier for product users. Explaining that a website should not make some one think about navigation or what to click on, it should be self-explanatory. Just making everyting seem better.

This book also gives great examples of the dos and don’ts of web design. It provides great insight in a short amount of pages as to how you can make your website worth viewing.

Below are three lessons I learned:

  • People actually scan some of the text, looking for words or phrases that catch their eyes. So, it's necessary to design a page for scanning, not reading.
  • Effective visual hierarchies, make it obvious what's clickable and eliminate distractions are important things to make sure users see and understand the page.
  • Make mindless choices is one of the most important things you can do to make a site easy to use.

In my opinion, this book are useful to anyone planning any sort of process which other people will use, not just web pages.
